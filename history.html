HISTORY

The band was formed in 1965, originally for an American TV series. The successful applicants were Dave Johns, Michael Lozenge, Micky Smithen and Pietro Dork. The band enjoyed immediate success with a string of hits such as Last Train to Clarkesville, Daydream Believer, I'm a Believer, Pleasant Valley Sunday and Little Bit Me, Little Bit You.

Pietro Dork left the band in 1969 to concentrate on his royal duties following his marriage to Princess Margarethe of Denmark.

The band finally split up in 1970 when Smithen left to apply for a place in the NASA Apollo space program. His application was successful and he spent the next 4 years in the Catering Department.

Dave Johns returned to horse racing and almost rode in the Welsh Grand National in 1974. He then created a successful donkey hire business empire in North Wales.

Michael Lozenge returned to his acting career touring with various amateur dramatic groups and has most recently appeared as a corpse in Casualty.

The band decided to reunite in 2010 and embarked on several critically acclaimed tours in the West of England, Wales and the Isle of Man.

They are constantly on the look out for new challenges, and are prepared to play almost anywhere and for any audience.
